Distinguishability-First Relational Cosmology

/

A research scaffold with a finite-dimensional theorem core, using admissible distinguishability, einselection, redundant records, and relevance-preserving compression to study classicality, identity, locality, and cosmological boundary behavior.

Abstract

I formulate a distinguishability-first research framework in which the proposed primitive is not spacetime geometry, observer-tagged measurement, or exact branch ontology, but covariant admissible distinguishability: the extent to which states can be discriminated by bounded local interactions. The paper has two layers. The first is a finite-dimensional theorem core: restricted measured distinguishability, budgeted admissibility, a worked noisy-qubit readout model, exact accessible-information thresholds in homogeneous and heterogeneous Quantum-Darwinism-style environments, branch-distance diagnostics, and controlled bottleneck/fixed-point constructions in commuting and finite-dimensional modular sectors. The second is a criterion-level cosmological program: record-rank, heat-death collapse of admissible records, Big-Bang-side factorization failure, emergent locality, black-hole boundary analogues, and cross-aeon structural compression. The research update made here is to state the mechanism stack explicitly: admissible interactions make distinctions available; einselection selects stable pointer structures; Quantum Darwinism proliferates redundant records; and relevance-preserving bottleneck flow is interpreted in RG language as a coupled flow of content and admissibility. The cosmological claims remain open targets, not completed derivations of our universe.

1. Introduction

The guiding claim of this framework is that the physically load-bearing primitive may be not geometry, exact branching, or observer-relative measurement, but the structure of which distinctions are physically realizable under bounded interactions. Spacetime locality, classical branches, stable identities, and cosmological cycle structure are then treated as candidate downstream organizations of admissible distinguishability. The finite-dimensional parts of the paper are intended as theorem-level scaffolding; the cosmological parts are explicitly criterion-level and remain open to failure.

Research integration

The framework should be read as a mechanism stack rather than a single metaphor. Decoherence suppresses interference; einselection identifies which states remain stable under environmental monitoring; Quantum Darwinism explains how those stable states become redundantly available in environment fragments; and the bottleneck/RG layer asks which structures remain relevant when inaccessible detail is quotiented away. This separation prevents the theory from overloading any one concept.

Load-bearing principle

The reader-mode formulation can be compressed into one rule: only admissibly distinguishable structure is physically load-bearing. Robust structure must appear as stable distinguishability, transport of distinguishability into records, or compression of distinguishability into a relevance-preserving finite summary. The paper mode formalizes this through \(\mathcal M_\epsilon\), \(\mathcal D_\epsilon\), record-rank, and admissibility-coupled bottleneck flow.

2. Primitive layer

Definition 2.1

A resource profile is \(\epsilon=(R,\tau,E,\nu,\delta)\), where \(R\) is a spacetime region, \(\tau\) is a proper-time budget, \(E\) is an interaction-energy or action budget, \(\nu\) is a bandwidth/resolution scale, and \(\delta\) is an allowed readout infidelity.

Definition 2.2

\(\mathcal M_\epsilon(R)\) is the set of instruments physically realizable in \(R\) under those bounds.

Definition 2.3
\[\mathcal D_\epsilon^{(R)}(\rho,\sigma):=\sup_{M\in\mathcal M_\epsilon(R)}D_{\rm KL}(p_\rho^M\|p_\sigma^M).\]
Axiom 2.4

Covariant admissibility. \(\mathcal M_\epsilon(R)\) is determined by interaction structure and causal support rather than observer tags.

Definition 2.5

\(\rho\sim_\epsilon \sigma\) iff \(\mathcal D_\epsilon^{(R)}(\rho,\sigma)=0\) for all admissible \(R\).

3. Budgeted admissibility and composability

Definition 3.1

Write the total budget as \(\bar\epsilon=\epsilon_c\oplus \epsilon_m\), where \(\epsilon_c\) is the channel budget and \(\epsilon_m\) is the measurement budget.

Definition 3.2

For the finite-dimensional qubit model, the channel budget is naturally expressed by the action \(K:=\int_0^\tau \|H(t)\|_\infty\,dt\).

Definition 3.3
\[\mathcal C_{\epsilon_c}=\left\{\Phi(\rho)=\operatorname{Tr}_A\!\big[U(\rho\otimes|0\rangle\!\langle0|_A)U^\dagger\big]:U=\mathcal T e^{-i\int_0^{\tau_c}H(t)dt},\int_0^{\tau_c}\|H(t)\|dt\le K_c\right\}.\]
Definition 3.4
\[\mathcal D_{\le \bar\epsilon}(\rho,\sigma):=\sup_{\epsilon_c\oplus\epsilon_m\preceq \bar\epsilon}\sup_{\Phi\in\mathcal C_{\epsilon_c}}\sup_{M\in\mathcal M_{\epsilon_m}}D_{\rm KL}(p_{\Phi(\rho)}^M\|p_{\Phi(\sigma)}^M).\]
Proposition 3.5
\[\mathcal D_{\le \bar\epsilon}(\Psi(\rho),\Psi(\sigma))\le\mathcal D_{\le \eta\oplus\bar\epsilon}(\rho,\sigma).\]

for every \(\Psi\in\mathcal C_\eta\).

Interpretive role

The budget split is what keeps the primitive from becoming an idealized all-POVM distinguishability. The channel budget and measurement budget are part of the theory's physical content: a distinction counts only to the extent that it survives under operations actually available to the regime. This is also what later makes an admissibility-coupled RG flow possible; after a quotient, the future channel class must be pushed forward rather than allowed to recover deleted detail for free.

7. Heterogeneous environments

Definition 7.1
\[C_F:=\prod_{i\in F} c_i\]
Proposition 7.2
\[I_{\rm acc}(F)\ge 1-\delta\iff C_F^2\le A_\delta.\]

This is the smallest size for which some fragment works.

Proposition 7.5

This is the smallest size for which every fragment works.

Corollary 7.6
\[m_\delta^{\rm best}\le m_\delta^{\rm worst}.\]

The interval between them is the partial-consensus window.

\[C_\delta^{\rm het}(m):=1-\frac{\operatorname{Var}_{|F|=m} I_{\rm acc}(F)}{H(X)^2}.\]

\[Q_{\epsilon,\delta}^{\rm frac,(N)}:=\frac1N\sum_{m=1}^N q_m,\qquad q_m:=\frac{1}{\binom{N}{m}}\sum_{|F|=m}\Theta\!\big(I_{\rm acc}(F)-(1-\delta)\big).\]
Corollary 7.9
\[Q_{\epsilon,\delta}^{\rm worst,(N)}\le Q_{\epsilon,\delta}^{\rm frac,(N)}\le Q_{\epsilon,\delta}^{\rm best,(N)}.\]
Corollary 7.10

In the homogeneous limit, all three heterogeneous order parameters collapse to the original homogeneous \(Q_{\epsilon,\delta}^{(N)}\).

8. Admissibility-coupled bottleneck flow: commuting sector and RG interpretation

Definition 8.1
\[X=(S,R),\qquad Y=f(S).\]
Convention 8.2

\(f\) acts as the identity on its image, so \(\Pi_Y^2=\Pi_Y\).

Definition 8.3
\[(\Pi)_*\mathfrak C:=\left\{\Psi\;\middle|\;\exists\,\Phi\in\mathfrak C,\;\Pi\circ\Phi=\Psi\circ\Pi\right\}.\]
Proposition 8.4

Under the iterated flow \(X\to S\to Y\to Y\cdots\), the minimal sufficient statistic \(Y\) is a nontrivial absorbing fixed point.

RG / information-bottleneck interpretation

The research reason to keep this section is that it gives a controlled version of the fixed-point idea. Ordinary compression does not generically produce a meaningful nontrivial attractor. The admissibility-coupled version is stronger: compression maps content forward while also pushing forward the admissible channel class. Directions quotiented out at one scale are no longer admissible distinctions at the next scale. In RG language, the surviving variable behaves like a relevance algebra; irrelevant directions are deleted, intermediate directions may survive one quotient, and relevant directions remain load-bearing.

\[ (\mathcal A_{k+1},\mathcal C_{k+1},\mathcal R_{k+1})\cong(\mathcal A_k,\mathcal C_k,\mathcal R_k)\quad\text{up to }\sim_\epsilon \]

This fixed-point criterion is not meant to replace the explicit commuting-sector proposition; it states the more general target suggested by the RG/information-bottleneck research pass.

Definition 16.5 — boundary kernel without a forced seed/physics split

The seed/physics distinction should not be forced before the transition map is known. The safer move is to define a boundary-relevance representative and then quotient it by boundary-admissible equivalence. The kernel is not literally asserted to be three separate substances; the three entries are roles that may appear as separate only from inside a successor regime.

\[ \widetilde Y_B := (\mathcal U_*,\lambda_{\rm rel},\chi_{\rm edge}), \qquad Y_B := [\widetilde Y_B]_{\sim_B}. \]

Here \(\mathcal U_*\) denotes successor universality or fixed-point structure, \(\lambda_{\rm rel}\) denotes finitely many relevant deformation coordinates, and \(\chi_{\rm edge}\) denotes minimal parent-child edge data. The equivalence relation \(\sim_B\) is boundary-admissible equivalence: two representatives are equivalent when no admissible comparison available at the boundary can distinguish them in any way that changes successor record-making capacity.

\[ \widetilde Y_B\sim_B \widetilde Y'_B \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad \mathcal D^{(B)}_{\epsilon_B}(\widetilde Y_B,\widetilde Y'_B)=0 \quad\text{for the boundary-admissible comparison class.} \]

When a successor regime has already formed, the kernel may admit inside-view projections,

\[ \pi_{\mathcal U}(Y_B)\simeq\mathcal U_*,\qquad \pi_{\lambda}(Y_B)\simeq\lambda_{\rm rel},\qquad \pi_{\chi}(Y_B)\simeq\chi_{\rm edge}, \]

but these projections should not be mistaken for proof that physics, seed values, and edge data are ontologically separate prior to the successor reconstruction map.

Open target 16.6

Identify a physically motivated boundary relevance algebra and boundary-admissible equivalence relation \(\sim_B\), then show that admissibility-coupled compression can converge to a nontrivial kernel \(Y_B\) without preserving microhistory and without collapsing to a vacuous equivalence class.

\[ (\mathcal A_k,\mathcal C_k,\mathcal R_k) \xrightarrow[k\to B]{\Pi_*} Y_B. \]
Decoder separation

Cross-boundary inheritance should keep \(\mathcal F\), \(Z^*\), and \(\mathcal I\) formally distinct: law, compressed structural seed, and decoder. This prevents the seed from being treated as a readable archive and supports the bounded-codomain structural-inheritance interpretation.

19. Closing

The framework is a research scaffold with a controlled mathematical core and a deliberately exposed conjectural frontier. The primitive is explicit enough to attack, the toy models yield exact thresholds and finite-\(N\) phase structure, the heterogeneous section gives a route toward imperfect records, and the modular sector identifies a controlled noncommuting domain. The current formulation separates the main mechanisms: einselection is the stability-selection mechanism, Quantum Darwinism is the public-record mechanism, RG-style bottleneck flow is the fixed-point mechanism candidate, and the boundary kernel is the formal target for cross-aeon inheritance.

Overview

DFRC asks how a universe becomes a place with usable facts, histories, identities, and boundaries when the only structure allowed to do that work is structure that can be physically distinguished.

The proposal is not that observers create reality, and it is not that anything outside present human measurement is unreal. The narrower claim is that a difference becomes load-bearing inside a regime only when admissible physical processes can register it, stabilize it, compare it, or pass it forward as a record.

The reader mode develops that claim in stages. First it explains how possible differences become admissible distinctions. Then it explains how distinctions become records, how records become redundant across fragments, how redundancy can support objectivity, and how branch-like classical history emerges only after those earlier conditions are in place.

The cosmological sections apply the same discipline to boundaries. Heat death, Big-Bang-side factorization failure, and black-hole horizon behavior are not treated as one identical event. They are compared because each stresses ordinary record-making in a different way. The guiding question is always the same: what kinds of distinction-making remain physically available?

Mechanism stack. The page uses this stack throughout. A distinction first has to be physically accessible; then stable pointer states have to be selected; then records have to proliferate; only then does branch-like classicality become a good description.

Neighboring theories and DFRC's difference

DFRC is not built by rejecting the surrounding literature. It borrows pressure from several nearby programs, but changes what is treated as primitive.

Standard decoherence

Decoherence explains why interference between alternatives becomes practically inaccessible when a system entangles with its environment. It is essential, but by itself it does not say that many independent observers can recover the same record from different fragments.

DFRC's use: decoherence is the suppression layer, not the whole theory of public reality.

Quantum Darwinism

Quantum Darwinism adds the replication layer: pointer-state information is copied into many environment fragments. This is much closer to DFRC because it explains how classical facts become public and redundant.

DFRC's use: Darwinian redundancy becomes one part of a larger admissibility-first account of branches, record-rank, and cosmological boundaries.

Conformal cyclic cosmology

CCC treats the remote future of one aeon and the Big Bang of the next as related through conformal structure, where scale loses its ordinary meaning.

DFRC's difference: DFRC does not start with conformal geometry. It asks whether the admissible means of registering, comparing, and stabilizing distinctions have failed on both sides of a boundary.

Baby universes and cosmological natural selection

Black-hole successor-universe ideas and cosmological natural selection already explore genealogical cosmology. They give DFRC a research neighborhood rather than a conclusion.

DFRC's difference: the proposed inheritance is not a full copy or ordinary signal. It is a boundary-admissible kernel that may preserve only universality, relevant coordinates, and local parent-child consistency.

DFRC

DFRC's primitive is admissible distinguishability: what differences can be physically registered, stabilized, copied, compared, or compressed by available interactions. From there it builds records, classicality, branches, time, locality, and boundary behavior.

Core claim: the same currency—admissible record-making capacity—should be used from quantum measurement through cosmological boundary claims.

1. Introduction: differences, records, and bridge terms

Most physical theories begin by granting some structure as basic: spacetime, fields, particles, a universal wavefunction, a preferred family of histories, or a pre-given ontology of branches. DFRC does not deny that those descriptions are useful. It asks whether they should be treated as the first explanatory layer when the problem is classicality, locality, identity, and cosmological history as seen from inside a universe.

The first question is operational, but not psychological. It is not “what does a human observer know?” It is: when can one physical state be told apart from another by processes the universe itself supports? A photon bath, a detector, a rock, a molecule, a gravitational imprint, a cell, or a brain can all participate if they carry stable correlations. The central issue is whether a possible difference can be converted into a durable record.

This reorders several familiar ideas. A branch is no longer a primitive world-line drawn into the ontology; it is a high-redundancy region of record structure. Locality is no longer assumed to be the first fact; it becomes tied to low-cost, high-fidelity exchange of records. Time is no longer treated as an external clock for the whole construction; it is the internal ordering of record production and comparison. A cosmological boundary is therefore not only a place where geometry becomes difficult. It is a place where the preconditions for record-making may fail.

The dependency chain is the spine of reader mode: possible difference → admissible distinction → stable record → redundant record → consensus-supporting objectivity → branch-like regime → cosmological record-rank → boundary failure or boundary inheritance.

Each later term is downstream of the earlier terms. If admissible distinctions fail, records cannot form. If records cannot stabilize, redundancy cannot accumulate. If redundancy does not support compatible access from fragments, objectivity does not arise. If objectivity does not arise, branch-like classical history is not yet a good description.

First law of the framework

Only admissibly distinguishable structure is physically load-bearing. That sentence is the cleanest way to state the primitive claim. A formal description can contain distinctions that no bounded physical process in the regime can register. DFRC does not need to call those distinctions unreal. It says they are not active structure for the physics available inside that regime.

Load-bearing structure appears in three forms: a stable distinction, the transport of a distinction into records, or the compression of distinction into a finite relevance-preserving summary. That is why the same concept can be used in small quantum measurement models and in the much more speculative boundary-kernel story without pretending those cases are equally proven.

The first picture: a difference has to climb into the world

DFRC uses the word difference in a deliberately physical way. A difference can be written into a mathematical description without yet being a difference that anything in the universe can use. To become part of a world with histories, the difference must become accessible to an interaction, then stable enough to leave a trace, then copied or preserved well enough that later systems can rely on it.

This is why the framework begins below words like “observer,” “world,” or “branch.” It first asks whether a physical process can turn a possible distinction into a record. A record does not have to be conscious or intentional. A scattered photon, a detector state, a chemical trace, a gravitational imprint, or a biological memory can all be records if they preserve a usable correlation.

possible differenceTwo descriptions differ on paper, but nothing has yet shown that the difference can be accessed.
admissible distinctionA bounded physical interaction can in principle make the difference show up in some readout.
stable recordThe readout persists long enough to affect later systems rather than vanishing immediately.
redundant recordMany fragments carry compatible traces, allowing independent recovery of the same fact.
branch-like regimeRecords are stable and separated enough that histories behave as effectively distinct sectors.

The ladder is not meant to imply that every physical process moves neatly through five literal stages. It is a way of keeping the dependencies clear: branch-like classicality is downstream of records, records are downstream of admissible interactions, and admissible interactions are downstream of real physical budgets.

A detector click

A particle leaves a mark only if the apparatus couples to the relevant degree of freedom, amplifies it, and preserves it against noise. Without that chain, the mathematical distinction was not a usable record.

A scattered photon

Light bouncing off an object can carry pointer-state information into the environment. Many scattered photons can make the same object-state recoverable from many different fragments.

A cosmological trace

A fossil signal matters only if it remains physically accessible. DFRC’s boundary claims ask what happens when the universe loses the conditions needed for such traces to be compared at all.

Known neighborhoods, not invented problems

DFRC uses some of its own names because it is trying to connect several areas at once. Those names should not hide their ancestry. Each one is meant to sit near an established problem, then extend it in a specific direction.

Admissible distinguishability is closest to restricted measurement distinguishability in quantum information: two states may be distinguishable in principle, but not under a restricted family of measurements. DFRC makes that restriction physical and regime-dependent. The admissible family is not merely a mathematical convenience; it encodes what bounded interactions can actually do inside the regime being described.

Record-rank is closest to Quantum Darwinist redundancy, accessible information in environment fragments, and the stronger objectivity conditions studied through strong Quantum Darwinism and spectrum broadcast structures. DFRC keeps the record-redundancy idea but generalizes it into a diagnostic for whether a whole regime has enough accessible, redundant, consensus-supporting witness structure.

Branch-regime sits near decoherent histories, consistent histories, and branching-state analyses. DFRC differs by refusing to treat a branch as a primitive metaphysical object. A branch-regime is a high-redundancy, high-consensus, low-interference region of record structure. It is a condition a state can approach, not a hard split assumed in advance.

Boundary kernel sits near renormalization-group and information-bottleneck thinking. The inherited object is not a complete diary of the previous universe. The better analogy is a fixed point plus finitely many relevant directions: the smallest surviving structure that can still generate a record-making successor regime, if such a successor transition exists.

The existing literature supplies local tools: restricted measurement families, einselection, redundant environmental records, histories frameworks, spectrum-broadcast objectivity, and RG-style relevance. DFRC’s distinctive move is to use admissible distinguishability as the common currency connecting those tools to cosmological boundaries.

The bridge terms also prevent an important misreading. DFRC is not inventing new problems to solve. It is asking whether several known problems are symptoms of one deeper issue: which differences can remain physically available as stable, shareable records under the admissibility conditions of a given regime?

2. Primitive layer

In conventional quantum information language, this section is about distinguishability under restricted measurements. DFRC uses the phrase admissible distinguishability for a reason: the restriction is not arbitrary. It is the set of measurements, channels, or instruments physically available under the regime’s own budgets.

So \(\mathcal D_\epsilon\) should be read as a physical question: if the universe grants only the resources encoded by \(\epsilon\), how far apart can two states be made to appear? The answer may change when energy, time, resolution, causal access, or readout reliability changes.

What admissible distinguishability actually means

A difference is a contrast in description. A distinction is stronger: it is a contrast that some physically allowed interaction can turn into different statistics, different records, or different future behavior. Admissible distinguishability is the amount of distinction available after the regime has paid all of its real physical costs.

The costs matter. The theory is not asking whether an impossible ideal observer could tell two states apart from outside the universe. It is asking what can be told apart by interactions that fit inside the available region, time budget, energy or action budget, bandwidth, causal access, and readout reliability. That is why the subscript in \(\mathcal D_\epsilon\) is not decoration: it means the answer can change when the physical access conditions change.

This also keeps the framework from becoming observer psychology. A photon bath, detector, molecule, black-hole horizon, or biological nervous system can participate in record-making if it carries stable correlations. Human knowledge is only one late and complicated instance of a more general physical capacity to make distinctions durable.

The primitive object is written as \(\mathcal D_\epsilon\). The notation is technical, but the idea is straightforward. Take two possible states of a system. Then ask: under the actual constraints of the situation — finite time, finite control strength, finite resolution, finite readout reliability, finite causal access — how distinguishable are they?

The resource profile \(\epsilon\) is there to block a very common mistake. If we allow every measurement that is mathematically definable, then distinguishability becomes an abstract ideal detached from physics. The point of the framework is the opposite: only distinctions that can be realized by physically allowed couplings are allowed to count.

That is why the primitive is not just “distance between states.” It is distinguishability under physical access conditions. This is one of the most important philosophical and mathematical moves in the theory.

The distinction between a mathematical measurement and an admissible measurement is essential. Quantum theory allows many measurements to be written down as formal objects. DFRC asks which of those measurements can be realized by bounded interactions in the regime under discussion. That is why \(\mathcal M_\epsilon\) matters: it is the gate between mathematical distinguishability and physical distinguishability.

Distinguishability in ordinary language

Two states are distinguishable when some allowed interaction can make them lead to different physical consequences. If a detector can reliably sort them into different outcomes, they are distinguishable for that detector and resource budget. If every physically available interaction produces the same statistics, then the two descriptions may still differ mathematically, but that difference is not active inside the regime being modeled.

This is why DFRC writes \(\mathcal D_\epsilon\) rather than just \(\mathcal D\). The subscript matters. It says that distinguishability is always being judged relative to access conditions: where the interaction can occur, how much energy and time it can use, how much bandwidth is available, and how reliable the readout is. A distinction that becomes visible only after granting impossible resources is not discarded as meaningless; it is classified as inadmissible for that regime.

The same idea scales upward. In the laboratory it describes noisy readout. In Quantum Darwinism it describes whether fragments carry accessible pointer-state information. In the cosmological sections it describes whether any stable witness structure remains at all.

Configuration-class equivalence: if every admissible comparison in a regime gives the same result for two candidate descriptions, then those descriptions may remain mathematically different but they are equivalent for the regime’s internal physics. This is the reader-mode version of \(\rho\sim_\epsilon\sigma\): sameness is judged by available distinguishability, not by hidden labels.

3. Budgeted admissibility

Real measurements cost something. They use time, interaction strength, apparatus complexity, and readout quality. Once that is admitted, another fact follows: preprocessing a state and then measuring it cannot be treated as free extra power.

The budgeted formalism makes this explicit. It splits the available resources into what is spent on dynamics and what is spent on measurement, then asks how distinguishability behaves under that split. This is less flashy than the cosmological claims, but it is one of the things that makes the framework more serious.

The measurement class is the gate

The symbol \(\mathcal M_\epsilon\) names the admissible instrument class: the measurements, channels, and readouts that can actually be built under the resource profile \(\epsilon\). If \(\mathcal M_\epsilon\) is too generous, the theory collapses into idealized mathematics. If it is too narrow, it throws away real physical distinctions. The research task is to construct it from interaction bounds rather than from observer labels.

In the worked qubit model, this is made concrete by allowing bounded interaction with an ancilla and noisy readout. That toy case does not solve cosmology, but it anchors the primitive: admissibility can be computed in an explicit model instead of merely named.

The operational takeaway is that the theory is trying to stay honest about physical cost. A distinction that only appears if one grants an impossible measuring setup does not count as fundamental here.

4. Worked qubit example

The qubit example is the first anchor. A qubit is the simplest nontrivial quantum system, so it is the natural place to show that the framework’s primitive object can actually be computed and is not just philosophical language.

The specific example uses a noisy pointer-basis measurement. The result is exactly what one hopes for: better readout means stronger distinguishability, worse readout means weaker distinguishability. In other words, the primitive behaves like a genuinely physical quantity.

This matters because the framework does not postpone all substance to the cosmology. It already has a calculable core at the smallest worked level.

5. Einselection and Quantum Darwinism model

Classical reality does not appear merely because a system interacts with an environment. Interaction first tends to suppress interference between alternatives; that is decoherence. But decoherence by itself does not yet explain why a particular set of alternatives becomes stable enough to function as classical states. That stability step is einselection: environmental monitoring favors pointer states, the states least disrupted by the monitoring interaction.

Quantum Darwinism adds the next step. A pointer state becomes objective-like when information about it is not stored in only one fragile place, but redundantly distributed across many environment fragments. If many different fragments can each answer the same question about the system, then many observers can independently arrive at the same description without disturbing the system directly. DFRC uses this as the bridge from admissible distinguishability to branch-like classicality.

This is where the framework becomes easiest to visualize. A small system interacts with many parts of its environment. If the interaction leaves strong enough imprints, then many different fragments of the environment each end up carrying information about the same coarse property of the system.

That is the basic insight of Quantum DarwinismA framework in which classical-looking facts emerge when many different fragments of the environment redundantly encode the same information about a system., and the theory uses it directly: classicality is not assumed — it is produced by redundant record structure.

In the toy model, the strength of imprinting is controlled by a single overlap parameter \(c\). If \(c\) is small, the two environment records are easy to tell apart. If \(c\) is close to 1, they overlap heavily and are hard to separate.

\[ c^{2N}\le A_\delta \]

This is the first result that should stick in the mind. It says there is an exact finite-\(N\) threshold for redundant record formation. In ordinary language: before fragments can act like many independent witnesses of the same fact, the environment as a whole must already separate the alternatives strongly enough. For the plots below, read the first graphic horizontally as “how much information do I get if I collect a fragment of size \(m\)?” and the second as “for a given overlap \(c\) and environment size \(N\), am I above or below the redundancy threshold?”

That gives the theory a true phase picture. Strong imprinting gives trivial classicality. Intermediate imprinting gives a meaningful redundancy regime. Weak imprinting means the environment never becomes a good witness, so operational classicality does not really switch on.

Objectivity is stronger than redundancy

Quantum Darwinism supplies the record-proliferation picture: many fragments of the environment can carry information about the same pointer state. DFRC needs that, but it also needs the stronger condition that those records support public agreement. A record is not enough if it is inaccessible, badly corrupted, or incompatible with the records carried by other fragments.

This is where bridge terms such as strong Quantum Darwinism and spectrum broadcast structure are useful. They name stricter versions of the same classicality problem: not merely whether information is copied, but whether independently accessible fragments contain compatible, effectively non-disturbing records. DFRC’s consensus and record-rank language should be understood in that stronger neighborhood.

6. Branching locus

A distinctive choice in the framework is that it does not treat branches as perfectly sharp pre-existing slices of reality. Instead, it defines a branching locusThe family of states close to stable, redundant record structure. In this theory, branching is proximity to that family rather than an exact split..

This matters because actual physical systems are messy. Record quality is finite, consensus can be partial, and classical structure often sharpens gradually rather than appearing all at once. The branching-locus picture captures that better than the image of a single exact split into worlds.

This is where DFRC should be read beside decoherent histories and consistent histories rather than only beside Many Worlds. Histories become classical-looking when interference is sufficiently suppressed and probability assignments become stable. DFRC adds a record-centered requirement: a branch-like regime is strongest when the relevant alternatives are not only decohered, but also supported by redundant, accessible, mutually compatible records.

So branching becomes a gradient. The more redundancy there is and the more stable the records are, the more branch-like the state becomes. At this stage, the branch-distance formulas are best understood as comparison scalings; a full distance-to-locus theorem requires a true minimization over the branch locus.

Why “how many worlds?” can be the wrong question: if branch individuation depends on redundant, compatible records, then branch sharpness can vary by scale, environment, and cosmic epoch. A branch-regime is not a clean tree limb appearing all at once; it is what a record structure approaches as redundancy rises, consensus improves, and interference becomes inaccessible.

7. Heterogeneous environments

Real environments are not uniform. Some pieces interact strongly with the system, some weakly, and some store cleaner records than others. The homogeneous model is therefore a useful baseline, but it is not the realistic case. A photon field, a gas, a detector array, or a cosmological environment will not usually copy the same record with identical strength into every fragment.

This matters because redundancy and objectivity can come apart. A single unusually good fragment may carry a usable record, while most fragments of the same size do not. In that case, the record exists, but it is not yet broadly public. DFRC therefore asks a more careful question than “does a fragment of size \(m\) work?” It asks what fraction of all size-\(m\) fragments work.

That fraction is written as \(q_m\). If \(q_m=0\), none of the fragments of that size clear the record threshold. If \(0<q_m<1\), witness structure is partial: some fragments carry usable records while others do not. If \(q_m=1\), every fragment of that size passes the threshold, so the information has become broadly available across the environment.

\[ q_m:=\frac{1}{\binom{N}{m}}\sum_{|F|=m}\Theta\!\big(I_{\rm acc}(F)-(1-\delta)H(X)\big), \qquad Q_{\epsilon,\delta}^{\rm frac,(N)}:=\frac1N\sum_{m=1}^N q_m. \]

In the binary equal-prior toy models used for the live graph, \(H(X)=1\), so the threshold becomes \(I_{\rm acc}(F)\ge 1-\delta\). The general expression keeps \(H(X)\) visible because, outside that toy model, the target record variable need not have one bit of entropy.

This is why the paper mode distinguishes best-case and worst-case fragment thresholds. The best threshold asks when some fragment can work. The worst threshold asks when every fragment of that size works. The gap between those two thresholds is the partial-consensus window.

\[ m_\delta^{\rm best}\le m_\delta^{\rm worst},\qquad Q_{\epsilon,\delta}^{\rm worst,(N)}\le Q_{\epsilon,\delta}^{\rm frac,(N)}\le Q_{\epsilon,\delta}^{\rm best,(N)}. \]

The conceptual move is that classical-looking record structure need not appear everywhere at once. In a heterogeneous environment, objectivity can grow gradually from no reliable witnesses, to partial agreement, to broad agreement across all fragments of a given size. This gives DFRC a way to talk about record structure that is neither purely private nor already fully public.

8. Admissibility-coupled RG bottleneck flow

The fixed-point problem is where DFRC asks how anything can survive a boundary without smuggling in a complete microscopic archive. The bridge here is renormalization-group and information-bottleneck reasoning: many microscopic details can wash out, while a smaller set of relevant variables remains capable of controlling large-scale behavior.

The DFRC addition is that compression alone is not enough. If a map quotients away a distinction, the later theory should not quietly allow that same deleted distinction to reappear as if it were still measurable. The admissible channel class has to flow with the compression. That is what the pushforward of admissibility is doing.

\[ (\Pi)_*\mathfrak C:=\left\{\Psi\;\middle|\;\exists\,\Phi\in\mathfrak C,\;\Pi\circ\Phi=\Psi\circ\Pi\right\}. \]

A simple commuting example is \(X=(S,R)\) with \(Y=f(S)\). The details in \(R\) are irrelevant to \(Y\); some information in \(S\) may be intermediate; the part captured by \(Y\) is relevant. After the flow reaches \(Y\), the surviving description is not merely smaller. It is smaller together with a smaller admissible measurement structure.

This is the reader-mode reason the paper mode treats the fixed point as a triple of algebra, channel class, and relevance structure rather than as a single compressed variable. The theory is not asking what information could be remembered by an impossible observer. It is asking what remains load-bearing after both description and admissibility have been pushed through the bottleneck.

Admissibility-coupled bottleneck flow

The point of the bottleneck is not just to compress. A good flow preserves the information relevant to the target description \(Y\); an over-compressive flow deletes that structure and cannot legitimately recover it later.

Connection to the math: the fixed point keeps the relevant content \(I(Z;Y)\) alive while the admissible channel class is pushed forward with the compression map.

9. Noncommuting modular sector

The commuting bottleneck picture is the cleanest place to explain the idea, but quantum theory is not generally commuting. Many observables cannot be treated as if they were ordinary simultaneously readable variables. That is why the noncommuting extension is restricted to a modularly admissible sector rather than claimed in full generality.

In the paper mode, this sector is described using finite-dimensional von Neumann algebras, a faithful state \(\omega\), and a descending chain of subalgebras invariant under the modular flow \(\sigma_t^\omega\). The important reader-mode point is not the technical machinery itself; it is why the machinery is needed. Conditional expectations in a noncommuting algebra are delicate. DFRC only uses them where they actually exist and preserve the relevant state.

Within that controlled setting, the chain stabilizes at a limiting algebra \(\mathcal B_\infty\), and the associated expectation becomes an idempotent projection onto the surviving fixed-point structure. The decomposition in paper mode is therefore a noncommuting analogue of separating what survives from what is discarded.

This section is deliberately cautious. It does not claim that every quantum cosmological situation has already been reduced to this finite-dimensional sector. It marks a safe mathematical foothold for the broader noncommuting program.

10. Intrinsic admissibility

Admissibility would be too weak if it meant “whatever an external super-observer is allowed to do.” DFRC’s embedded-observer principle says that the measuring structures must be buildable from the same macrostate family being described. A universe does not get to borrow an apparatus from outside itself.

This does not mean only actually performed measurements count. The relevant notion is counterfactual admissibility: what the regime could physically support as measurement, comparison, and record-making structure. A world may contain no one currently performing a measurement, while still having the resources to support that measurement in principle.

That is why paper mode gives each macrostate an intrinsic profile \(\epsilon_{\rm int}(\rho)\). The profile tracks available region, time, interaction strength, bandwidth or complexity, and readout fidelity as features of the regime itself. The continuity assumptions prevent tiny changes of state from causing arbitrary jumps in what counts as physically admissible.

In reader-mode terms, intrinsic admissibility is the guardrail that keeps DFRC from becoming either pure observer-relativism or pure ideal-measurement Platonism. Measurement capacity is physical, embedded, and regime-dependent.

11. Cosmological record-rank

Record-rank is DFRC’s regime-level diagnostic for witness capacity. It asks whether admissible observables can be recovered from environment fragments with enough accessible information to support a shared description. It is inspired by Quantum Darwinist redundancy, but it is not identical to a single standard redundancy number.

\[ Q_{\epsilon,\delta}(\rho):=\sup_{X\in\mathsf O_\epsilon}\int_0^1 df\,\Theta\!\Big(\bar I_{\rm acc}^\epsilon(X:F_f)-(1-\delta)H(X)\Big). \]

The distinction from ordinary redundancy matters. A regime can have many correlations that are not accessible. It can have accessible fragments that disagree. It can have redundant information that is too noisy to support consensus. Stronger objectivity ideas, such as strong Quantum Darwinism and spectrum broadcast structure, are useful bridges here because they emphasize that public reality requires more than “many copies.” The records must be accessible, mutually compatible, and effectively non-disturbing.

Record-rank also needs to stay separate from fragment threshold, redundancy, and consensus variance. A fragment threshold asks how large a fragment must be. Redundancy asks how many such fragments exist. Consensus asks whether those fragments agree. Record-rank is the broader DFRC diagnostic that tries to track when the whole witness structure is available enough for classicality.

12. Heat death

In ordinary thermodynamic language, heat death means exhausted gradients. DFRC adds an informational reading: the universe can lose the physical conditions needed to maintain redundant accessible records of the observables that make a shared classical description possible.

Heat death is darkness, not necessarily simple uniformity: the important DFRC claim is the loss of usable gradients, detectors, and witness architecture. There may still be a formal state description, but if no embedded structure can maintain reliable comparisons, record-rank collapses because records are no longer recoverable as public witnesses.

The controlled theorem is narrower than the cosmological story. In a finite-dimensional relaxation setting, with trivial zero-entropy observables excluded, \(Q_{\epsilon,\delta}\) tends to zero. The cosmological version still needs energy-constrained continuity and a precise causal-patch or local-algebra formulation.

The schematic below should therefore be read as an explanation of the logic, not as observational data. If usable measurement budgets collapse and readout infidelity rises, then reliable fragments stop being recoverable. In that situation, record-rank goes to zero because the environment no longer functions as a stable witness architecture.

13. Big Bang-side vanishing

The early-boundary claim is subtler than the late one. The theory does not say the Big Bang is simply the same as heat death. It says that near the early boundary, the subsystem structure needed to define fragments may fail altogether.

If fragments cannot be meaningfully separated, then the setup required for record redundancy never comes online. In that sense, \(Q\) can vanish at the beginning for a different reason than it vanishes at the end.

This is the heart of the framework’s phase-equivalence idea: beginning and end may converge as limits of vanishing record structure without becoming the same geometric state. This is a criterion-level claim rather than a derived early-universe model; its mathematical burden is to make the split-scale story precise in algebraic quantum field theory or an equivalent local-algebra framework.

14. Emergent locality

This is one of the boldest claims in the framework, so it has to be explained carefully. The idea is not that space is an illusion. It is that the useful notion of nearness may be reconstructed from patterns of physically available correlation and record exchange rather than assumed from the start.

Locality as stable record exchange

Near and far are usually treated as geometric ideas. DFRC asks whether geometry can be read as a summary of record exchange. Two systems are effectively near when they can exchange and stabilize records cheaply, reliably, and repeatedly. They are effectively far when that exchange is costly, noisy, delayed, or unavailable.

This does not yet derive spacetime. It gives the route: reconstruct effective neighborhoods from low-cost, high-fidelity admissible distinguishability relations. Geometry is then a coarse summary of stable distinguishability neighborhoods, not the first explanatory layer.

A good lay analogy is a map reconstructed from travel times rather than from an aerial photograph. If city A can exchange people, goods, and information with city B very quickly, but only very slowly with city C, then even without seeing a literal map one can begin to infer which cities are effectively near each other. The framework proposes something similar for physics: systems that can strongly and reliably stabilize each other’s records count as near in an emergent sense, while systems whose correlations are weak, delayed, or inaccessible count as far apart.

In formal language, the theory tries to build a notion of neighborhood out of admissible distinguishability patterns: which subsystems can exchange robust information, how strongly they are linked, and whether those links can be organized into something that behaves like a low-dimensional geometry.

When the framework says locality could be “reconstructed,” it means something specific: one tries to recover the effective shape of space from the structure of record-bearing relations, rather than taking the spatial background as primitive from the outset. The toy result assumes strong exponential-weight structure, so it is not a derivation of locality from nothing. It is a sanity check and a target for a harder theorem.

To read the graph below, watch for three ingredients at once. First, thick links mean two subsystems can support one another’s records strongly enough to behave as near neighbors. Second, thin cross-links mean correlation still exists, but not strongly enough to organize a stable shared neighborhood. Third, clusters form because many strong links reinforce one another, so “place” begins to emerge as a property of the whole network rather than as an input background.

15. Cosmological regimes

The theory does not assume one single measurement capacity across all of cosmic history. Different eras should have different intrinsic admissibility profiles.

Reheating matters because it is the first obvious regime in which ordinary matter sectors, scattering, and record-bearing interactions become broadly available. A late de Sitter-like regime matters because it is the natural place where usable non-equilibrium budgets and readout fidelity may collapse.

So the cosmological story here is not one universal bridge. It is a family of bridges, each tied to a specific regime and its own measurement structure.

Black holes as local boundary analogues

In the full cosmological extension, black holes are not decorative. They are the most important local case where ordinary access to interior structure breaks down and the exterior description becomes boundary-centered. DFRC should not say that the infalling description is simply false. The safer statement is that the external admissible description is horizon-bound: what can be registered, stabilized, and later recovered is associated with boundary degrees of freedom and outgoing radiation rather than direct inspection of an interior.

This makes black holes natural candidates for a successor-universe module, but not a proven mechanism. The finite-dimensional theorem core does not depend on black holes. The stronger cosmological genealogy does.

16. Boundary conditions and the boundary kernel

The boundary kernel should be read through the bridge of renormalization-group and information-bottleneck reasoning. In RG language, most microscopic details are irrelevant at a fixed point; what remains is a universality structure plus a finite number of relevant directions. DFRC adapts that shape to cosmological inheritance: the successor does not need a complete memory of the parent, but it cannot be explanatory nothing either.

This is why the notation separates \(\widetilde Y_B\) from \(Y_B\). The tuple names a representative structure. The quotient says only boundary-admissibly distinguishable content counts as inherited structure. The kernel is therefore not “the seed” in an ordinary archival sense. It is the finite successor-relevant equivalence class, if such a boundary map exists.

The boundary-kernel idea exists because the theory needs a more careful answer than “the seed survives” or “the physics survives.” At a cosmological boundary, the full prior microhistory cannot be treated as a recoverable archive. If the late universe has lost the admissible means to compare and stabilize most distinctions, then the surviving object cannot be a complete diary of what happened. But total erasure is also too strong. A successor universe, if such a thing is possible, cannot be generated by nothing in the explanatory sense. Something must play the role of a minimal inherited structure.

DFRC therefore separates a representative object from its equivalence class. The representative is written as \(\widetilde Y_B=(\mathcal U_*,\lambda_{\rm rel},\chi_{\rm edge})\). The actual boundary kernel is not that tuple as a naked object; it is the tuple considered only up to boundary admissible indistinguishability:

\[ \widetilde Y_B=(\mathcal U_*,\lambda_{\rm rel},\chi_{\rm edge}), \qquad Y_B=[\widetilde Y_B]_{\sim_B}. \]

This notation matters. \(\mathcal U_*\) is the universality or fixed-point structure that would appear from inside the successor as law-like physics. \(\lambda_{\rm rel}\) are finite relevant coordinates: values, deformations, or initial-condition biases that remain important after irrelevant detail has been compressed away. \(\chi_{\rm edge}\) is the parent-child edge datum: not a full memory of the parent, but the minimum relation that prevents the successor from being treated as source-free. The quotient \([\cdot]_{\sim_B}\) says that distinctions which cannot survive the boundary admissibility relation should not be counted as separate inherited facts.

Seed, law, and decoder must stay distinct

The older fork had a strength worth restoring: reconstruction is only meaningful if three roles are kept separate. \(\mathcal F\) names the dynamical law, \(Z^*\) names the compressed structural seed, and \(\mathcal I\) names the inference map or decoder. If these are blurred together, the theory can accidentally pretend that having a seed is the same as being able to read it.

This supports the current bounded-seed framing. Cross-aeon inheritance is not a detailed archive of everything that happened. It is, at most, structural inheritance: the finite information needed to specify what kind of record-making successor regime can be generated, if such a boundary map exists.

17. Falsification

A serious theory must be able to lose. This framework can fail in clear ways: if admissibility secretly depends on observers, if no stable branching locus exists, if compression only yields trivial fixed points, if cosmological boundaries do not actually drive record-rank to zero, or if the supposed residual channel cannot be distinguished from noise.

In other words, the theory is trying to move out of the realm of suggestive language and into the realm of breakable claims.

Core, conjectural center, optional phenomenology

The controlled core is the finite-dimensional distinguishability scaffold: admissible measurement classes, explicit toy distinguishability, record redundancy thresholds, branch-distance diagnostics, and record-rank. The conjectural center is the cosmological extension: boundary equivalence, fixed-point compression, inside/outside projection, and structural inheritance. Optional phenomenology, such as primordial black holes or residual-constant channels, can fail without killing the core.

This separation is important because it keeps DFRC breakable. If \(\mathcal M_\epsilon\) cannot be built from physical bounds, the primitive fails. If no stable branching locus can be defined from redundancy and consensus, the branch program fails. If bottleneck flow only gives trivial fixed points, the cross-aeon program fails. Those are not rhetorical weaknesses; they are the framework’s stress tests.

19. Closing

The shortest statement of the proposal is this: reality becomes classical where differences can be copied into many stable records, and it loses classical structure where that record-making capacity disappears.

What makes the framework promising is not completeness. It is that its central claims are clear enough to be criticized, extended, tested, and perhaps eventually falsified.
