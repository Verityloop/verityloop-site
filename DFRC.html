Distinguishability-First Relational Cosmology

/

A research scaffold with a finite-dimensional theorem core, built around the claim that physically admissible distinguishability may be more primitive than the classical structures that emerge from it.

Abstract

I formulate a distinguishability-first research framework in which the proposed primitive is not spacetime geometry, observer-tagged measurement, or exact branch ontology, but covariant admissible distinguishability: the extent to which states can be discriminated by bounded local interactions. The paper currently has two different layers. The first is a finite-dimensional theorem core: restricted measured distinguishability, budgeted admissibility, a worked noisy-qubit readout model, exact accessible-information thresholds in homogeneous and heterogeneous Quantum-Darwinism-style environments, and controlled bottleneck/fixed-point constructions in commuting and finite-dimensional modular sectors. The second is a criterion-level cosmological program: record-rank, heat-death collapse of admissible records, Big-Bang-side factorization failure, emergent locality, and cross-aeon structural compression. Those cosmological claims are not presented here as completed derivations of our universe. They are structured open targets whose value is that they make the speculative parts precise enough to test, repair, or falsify.

1. Introduction

The guiding claim of this framework is that the physically load-bearing primitive may be not geometry, exact branching, or observer-relative measurement, but the structure of which distinctions are physically realizable under bounded interactions. Spacetime locality, classical branches, stable identities, and cosmological cycle structure are then treated as candidate downstream organizations of admissible distinguishability. The finite-dimensional parts of the paper are intended as theorem-level scaffolding; the cosmological parts are explicitly criterion-level and remain open to failure.

Overview

This theory starts from a different place than most cosmology. Instead of asking first what the universe is made of, it asks what differences the universe can physically detect, preserve, and pass along. The framework is a disciplined speculative program with a calculable quantum-information core and a bolder question: whether classical reality, locality, and cosmic boundary behavior can be organized around record-making capacity.

Status: finite-dimensional theorem core + open cosmological extension

That matters because many of the hardest problems in physics appear exactly where ordinary classical description stops working: measurement, branching, objectivity, the role of observers, and the relation between the beginning and end of cosmic history. The framework says these may become easier to organize if we stop treating the familiar world as basic and instead ask how it is produced from distinguishabilityHow well two physical situations can actually be told apart by the interactions and measurement resources available in that setting..

The core picture is simple enough to say in everyday language: a fact becomes real in the strong practical sense when many parts of the world can independently carry and stabilize that fact. If that copying process is weak, the world stays more quantum and less classically definite. If that copying process collapses, then the ordinary world of stable shared facts dissolves with it.

1. Introduction

Most theories begin by choosing some basic furniture of reality: particles, fields, wavefunctions, spacetime, or something like them. This framework does not deny those things matter. What it questions is whether they are the best starting point for understanding why the universe appears classical, local, and history-bearing from the inside.

The theory instead begins with a practical physical question: when can one state of the world be told apart from another by processes the universe itself can support? That is why admissibilityThe rule that only physically realizable measurements and interventions count, not every measurement that is mathematically imaginable. comes before branches, before identity, and before cycle structure. The framework is strongest where that idea is turned into finite-dimensional calculations, and most speculative where it is projected onto cosmology.

This is not a claim about human knowledge in particular. A detector, a crystal defect, a biological memory, a radiation imprint, or a field fragment can all count as carriers of physical difference if they preserve and transmit it. The framework is therefore about what the world can do with distinctions, not about what people happen to know.

2. Primitive layer

The primitive object is written as \(\mathcal D_\epsilon\). The notation is technical, but the idea is straightforward. Take two possible states of a system. Then ask: under the actual constraints of the situation — finite time, finite control strength, finite resolution, finite readout reliability, finite causal access — how distinguishable are they?

The resource profile \(\epsilon\) is there to block a very common mistake. If we allow every measurement that is mathematically definable, then distinguishability becomes an abstract ideal detached from physics. The point of the framework is the opposite: only distinctions that can be realized by physically allowed couplings are allowed to count.

That is why the primitive is not just “distance between states.” It is distinguishability under physical access conditions. This is one of the most important philosophical and mathematical moves in the theory.

3. Budgeted admissibility

Real measurements cost something. They use time, interaction strength, apparatus complexity, and readout quality. Once that is admitted, another fact follows: preprocessing a state and then measuring it cannot be treated as free extra power.

The budgeted formalism makes this explicit. It splits the available resources into what is spent on dynamics and what is spent on measurement, then asks how distinguishability behaves under that split. This is less flashy than the cosmological claims, but it is one of the things that makes the framework more serious.

For a non-specialist, the takeaway is simple: the theory is trying to stay honest about physical cost. A distinction that only appears if one grants an impossible measuring setup does not count as fundamental here.

4. Worked qubit example

The qubit example is the first anchor. A qubit is the simplest nontrivial quantum system, so it is the natural place to show that the framework’s primitive object can actually be computed and is not just philosophical language.

The specific example uses a noisy pointer-basis measurement. The result is exactly what one hopes for: better readout means stronger distinguishability, worse readout means weaker distinguishability. In other words, the primitive behaves like a genuinely physical quantity.

This matters because the framework does not postpone all substance to the cosmology. It already has a calculable core at the smallest worked level.

5. Homogeneous Darwinism model

This is where the framework becomes easiest to visualize. A small system interacts with many parts of its environment. If the interaction leaves strong enough imprints, then many different fragments of the environment each end up carrying information about the same coarse property of the system.

That is the basic insight of Quantum DarwinismA framework in which classical-looking facts emerge when many different fragments of the environment redundantly encode the same information about a system., and the theory uses it directly: classicality is not assumed — it is produced by redundant record structure.

In the toy model, the strength of imprinting is controlled by a single overlap parameter \(c\). If \(c\) is small, the two environment records are easy to tell apart. If \(c\) is close to 1, they overlap heavily and are hard to separate.

\[ c^{2N}\le A_\delta \]

This is the first result that should stick in the mind. It says there is an exact finite-\(N\) threshold for redundant record formation. In ordinary language: before fragments can act like many independent witnesses of the same fact, the environment as a whole must already separate the alternatives strongly enough. For the plots below, read the first graphic horizontally as “how much information do I get if I collect a fragment of size \(m\)?” and the second as “for a given overlap \(c\) and environment size \(N\), am I above or below the redundancy threshold?”

That gives the theory a true phase picture. Strong imprinting gives trivial classicality. Intermediate imprinting gives a meaningful redundancy regime. Weak imprinting means the environment never becomes a good witness, so operational classicality does not really switch on.

6. Branching locus

A distinctive choice in the framework is that it does not treat branches as perfectly sharp pre-existing slices of reality. Instead, it defines a branching locusThe family of states close to stable, redundant record structure. In this theory, branching is proximity to that family rather than an exact split..

This matters because actual physical systems are messy. Record quality is finite, consensus can be partial, and classical structure often sharpens gradually rather than appearing all at once. The branching-locus picture captures that better than the image of a single exact split into worlds.

So branching becomes a gradient. The more redundancy there is and the more stable the records are, the more branch-like the state becomes. At this stage, the branch-distance formulas are best understood as comparison scalings; a full distance-to-locus theorem requires a true minimization over the branch locus.

8. Commuting bottleneck flow

Within a single era, the world becomes classically readable when differences become stable because the environment records them redundantly. The next question appears once one starts thinking cosmologically. If a universe runs all the way toward a record-poor end state, and if the framework is serious about cycle-closure or cross-aeon inheritance, then what exactly could survive in compressed form?

That is why compression enters the story. The issue is not “compress for convenience.” It is: if most late-universe detail is irrelevant to what the next aeon can become, is there a principled way to keep only the part that still matters? The commuting bottleneck flow is the cleanest mathematical prototype of that idea.

In this simplified setting, one splits information into what is relevant for future structure and what is not. Repeated relevance-preserving compression then pushes the description toward a nontrivial fixed point: not total erasure, but a smaller description that still retains the part that matters for what comes next.

The point is not technical elegance by itself. The commuting bottleneck flow provides a mathematically controlled version of the claim that a universe might not need to carry all of its microscopic detail forward — only a compressed inheritance structure that still preserves the information relevant to the next stage of cosmic history.

9. Modular noncommuting sector

The previous section used the mathematically friendlier case: a setting where the relevant pieces of information can be treated as if they fit together cleanly. Real quantum theory is often harder than that. Different observables can interfere with each other, fail to be jointly readable, or refuse to sit inside one tidy classical description.

The noncommuting sector asks whether the same compression idea works when the information one wants to keep is genuinely quantum and not classically compatible. The answer is limited but meaningful: the controlled theorem lives in a restricted regime where the mathematics behaves cleanly.

That restricted regime is called the modularly admissible sectorA limited quantum regime where the mathematical tools needed for the compression theorem work cleanly. Beyond that regime, the full theorem remains open.. For a non-specialist, the right way to read that phrase is not “here is more jargon to learn,” but “this is the part of the quantum problem where the framework has a controlled result.”

This marks the boundary between controlled result and open problem. The full quantum compression problem remains open, but the framework identifies a domain in which the structure survives and can be rigorously controlled.

10. Intrinsic admissibility

One of the theory’s most important physical constraints is the Embedded Observer PrincipleThe rule that observers and measuring devices must be built from the same universe they are measuring, rather than borrowed from outside it.. That means you do not get to import a perfect clock or detector from outside the universe whenever it is convenient.

The intrinsic admissibility profile \(\epsilon_{\rm int}\) is the formal way of describing what kinds of measurements a given cosmological regime can support from within itself. This is why the framework can talk seriously about access, screening, and observational limitations without falling back into vague mysticism.

The component-wise transport law matters because it keeps that internal measurement capacity from fluctuating arbitrarily under the very dynamics it is meant to describe.

11. Record-rank

The quantity \(Q\) is not a count of worlds, and it is not identical to standard Quantum Darwinism redundancy. In this framework it is a witness-capacity diagnostic: it asks how much above-threshold record structure remains accessible under the chosen physical limits.

In the homogeneous toy model, \(Q\) can be read very concretely. Once the threshold fragment size \(m_\delta\) is known, every fragment size \(m\ge m_\delta\) counts as above threshold, while smaller fragments do not. For finite \(N\), that gives the stepwise expression

\[ Q_{\epsilon,\delta}^{(N)}=\frac{N-m_\delta+1}{N}\quad (m_\delta\le N), \qquad Q_{\epsilon,\delta}^{(N)}=0\quad (m_\delta>N). \]

A high \(Q\) means the environment has many usable witness sizes; a low \(Q\) means the records are sparse or require nearly the whole environment to recover. The graph keeps the staircase form because \(m_\delta\) is an integer threshold.

What this graph measures: as the overlap \(c\) increases, environment records become less distinguishable, so the threshold fragment size grows and the record-rank \(Q\) falls.

Why it is stepped: the curve changes only when the integer threshold \(m_\delta\) changes. The staircase is therefore part of the finite-\(N\) mathematics, not a visual artifact.

12. Heat death

In ordinary thermodynamic language, heat death means exhausted gradients. DFRC adds an informational reading: the universe can lose the physical conditions needed to maintain redundant accessible records of the observables that make a shared classical description possible.

The controlled theorem is narrower than the cosmological story. In a finite-dimensional relaxation setting, with trivial zero-entropy observables excluded, \(Q_{\epsilon,\delta}\) tends to zero. The cosmological version still needs energy-constrained continuity and a precise causal-patch or local-algebra formulation.

The schematic below should therefore be read as an explanation of the logic, not as observational data. If usable measurement budgets collapse and readout infidelity rises, then reliable fragments stop being recoverable. In that situation, record-rank goes to zero because the environment no longer functions as a stable witness architecture.

13. Big Bang-side vanishing

The early-boundary claim is subtler than the late one. The theory does not say the Big Bang is simply the same as heat death. It says that near the early boundary, the subsystem structure needed to define fragments may fail altogether.

If fragments cannot be meaningfully separated, then the setup required for record redundancy never comes online. In that sense, \(Q\) can vanish at the beginning for a different reason than it vanishes at the end.

This is the heart of the framework’s phase-equivalence idea: beginning and end may converge as limits of vanishing record structure without becoming the same geometric state. This is a criterion-level claim rather than a derived early-universe model; its mathematical burden is to make the split-scale story precise in algebraic quantum field theory or an equivalent local-algebra framework.

14. Emergent locality

This is one of the boldest claims in the framework, so it has to be explained carefully. The idea is not that space is an illusion. It is that the useful notion of nearness may be reconstructed from patterns of physically available correlation and record exchange rather than assumed from the start.

A good lay analogy is a map reconstructed from travel times rather than from an aerial photograph. If city A can exchange people, goods, and information with city B very quickly, but only very slowly with city C, then even without seeing a literal map one can begin to infer which cities are effectively near each other. The framework proposes something similar for physics: systems that can strongly and reliably stabilize each other’s records count as near in an emergent sense, while systems whose correlations are weak, delayed, or inaccessible count as far apart.

In formal language, the theory tries to build a notion of neighborhood out of admissible distinguishability patterns: which subsystems can exchange robust information, how strongly they are linked, and whether those links can be organized into something that behaves like a low-dimensional geometry.

When the framework says locality could be “reconstructed,” it means something specific: one tries to recover the effective shape of space from the structure of record-bearing relations, rather than taking the spatial background as primitive from the outset. The toy result assumes strong exponential-weight structure, so it is not a derivation of locality from nothing. It is a sanity check and a target for a harder theorem.

15. Cosmological regimes

The theory does not assume one single measurement capacity across all of cosmic history. Different eras should have different intrinsic admissibility profiles.

Reheating matters because it is the first obvious regime in which ordinary matter sectors, scattering, and record-bearing interactions become broadly available. A late de Sitter-like regime matters because it is the natural place where usable non-equilibrium budgets and readout fidelity may collapse.

So the cosmological story here is not one universal bridge. It is a family of bridges, each tied to a specific regime and its own measurement structure.

16. Cross-aeon compression

The role of compression is central to the cycle question: if the universe approaches a regime where ordinary record structure dissolves, then any cycle picture has to answer a hard question — what, if anything, could carry forward without requiring an impossible archive of everything that ever happened?

The seed is not treated as an unconstrained archive of everything that happened. Such an archive would demand implausible channel capacity and blur the framework into fantasy.

The seed is restricted to a bounded space of effective-theory structures \(\mathcal T_B(U)\). In ordinary language: what carries across aeons is not a complete diary. It is, at most, a compressed inheritance object describing what kind of universe can be rebuilt.

This bounded-seed view makes the cross-aeon claim smaller, but far more defensible. It also marks the boundary clearly: cross-aeon inheritance is a formal scaffold and research target, not an established physical mechanism.

17. Falsification

A serious theory must be able to lose. This framework can fail in clear ways: if admissibility secretly depends on observers, if no stable branching locus exists, if compression only yields trivial fixed points, if cosmological boundaries do not actually drive record-rank to zero, or if the supposed residual channel cannot be distinguished from noise.

In other words, the theory is trying to move out of the realm of suggestive language and into the realm of breakable claims.

19. Closing

The shortest statement of the proposal is this: reality becomes classical where differences can be copied into many stable records, and it loses classical structure where that record-making capacity disappears.

What makes the framework promising is not completeness. It is that its central claims are clear enough to be criticized, extended, tested, and perhaps eventually falsified.
